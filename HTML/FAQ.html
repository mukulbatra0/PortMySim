Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is SIM porting?
SIM porting, also known as mobile number portability (MNP), allows you to switch to a new mobile network provider while keeping your existing phone number.
2. How long does the SIM porting process take?
The SIM porting process typically takes 1-2 business days to complete, depending on your current provider and the new network.
3. Can I port my number to any network?
Yes, you can port your number to any network that supports mobile number portability (MNP). However, you must ensure that your current number is eligible for porting.
4. Will I experience any service disruption during porting?
There may be a brief service interruption (usually a few hours) during the porting process. We recommend scheduling the porting at a convenient time.
5. What information do I need to port my number?
You will need your current mobile number, a valid ID proof, and your current network's customer details. You may also need a Porting Authorization Code (PAC) from your current provider.
6. Can I cancel my porting request?
Yes, you can cancel your porting request before the process is completed. Contact your new provider to cancel the request.
7. Is there a fee for SIM porting?
Some providers may charge a nominal fee for SIM porting. Please check with your new network provider for details.
8. What happens to my old SIM card after porting?
Your old SIM card will be deactivated once the porting process is complete. You will receive a new SIM card from your new provider.
9. Can I port my number if I have an outstanding balance?
You must clear any outstanding balance with your current provider before initiating the porting process.
10. How do I check the status of my porting request?
You can check the status of your porting request by contacting your new network provider or visiting their website.
